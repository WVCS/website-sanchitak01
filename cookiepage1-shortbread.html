    SHORTBREAD COOKIES
    3 ½ cups all-purpose flour
    1 ½ cups unsalted butter, room temperature
    1 cup sugar, extra for sprinkling
    1 cup (6 oz) of semisweet finely chopped chocolate
    1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    ¼ teaspoon salt


    INSTRUCTIONS

    1. In the bowl of an electric mixer fitted with a paddle attachment, mix together the butter and 1 cup of sugar until they are just combined. Add the vanilla.

    2. In a medium bowl, sift together the flour and salt, then add them to the butter-and-sugar mixture. Mix on low speed until the dough starts to come together.

    3. Dump onto a surface dusted with flour and shape into a flat disk. Wrap in plastic and chill for 30 minutes.

    4. Roll the dough 1/2-inch thick and cut with a 3 by 1-inch finger-shaped cutter. Place the cookies on an ungreased baking sheet and sprinkle with sugar. Bake for 20 to 25 minutes, until the edges begin to brown.

    5. Allow to cool to room temperature. When the cookies are cool, place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Put 3 ounces of the chocolate in a glass bowl and microwave on high power for 30 seconds.

    6. Stir with a wooden spoon, making it glossier. Continue to heat and stir in 30-second increments until the chocolate is just melted. Sit at room temperature, stirring often, until completely smooth and slightly cooled.

    7. Coat half the cookie with the chocolate, and set in refrigerator to harden.
